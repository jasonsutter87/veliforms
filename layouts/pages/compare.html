Why choose VeilForms?

1

True Privacy, Not Theater

Other form builders encrypt data on their servers. We encrypt it in your users' browsers. We literally cannot read your data—and neither can anyone who breaches our systems.

2

Use Your Own Forms

Keep your existing form HTML. Just add our SDK and a data attribute. No need to recreate forms in a proprietary builder or learn a new tool.

Detailed Comparisons

VeilForms vs Typeform

Typeform pioneered beautiful, conversational forms. But beauty doesn't equal security.

  • Security: Typeform uses server-side encryption—they hold the keys and can access your data. VeilForms encrypts in the browser; we never see plaintext.
  • Tracking: Typeform includes analytics and can share data with third parties. VeilForms has zero tracking.
  • Flexibility: Typeform requires using their builder. VeilForms works with any HTML form.
  • Price: Typeform starts at $25/mo with just 10 responses on free. VeilForms starts at $19/mo with 3 full forms free.

Choose Typeform if: Design aesthetics are your top priority and you don't handle sensitive data.

Choose VeilForms if: Privacy and security matter, or you need HIPAA-grade protection.

VeilForms vs JotForm

JotForm offers powerful features and integrations. But with power comes complexity—and privacy concerns.

  • Security: JotForm's encryption is server-side. Their team can access your data. HIPAA compliance requires an expensive add-on.
  • Tracking: JotForm collects extensive analytics and user data.
  • Complexity: JotForm's 400+ templates and 100+ integrations can be overwhelming. VeilForms keeps it simple.
  • Price: JotForm's Bronze plan starts at $34/mo. VeilForms Pro is $19/mo.

Choose JotForm if: You need extensive integrations and don't mind the complexity.

Choose VeilForms if: Security-first design and simplicity appeal to you.

VeilForms vs Palform

Palform is the closest competitor—they also offer client-side encryption. Here's how we differ.

  • PII Detection: VeilForms automatically detects and flags sensitive data. Palform doesn't.
  • Anonymous IDs: VeilForms uses cryptographic anonymous IDs. Palform uses standard identifiers.
  • Flexibility: Palform requires their form builder. VeilForms works with any HTML.
  • Tracking: Palform has some analytics. VeilForms has zero tracking.

Choose Palform if: You want open-source and self-hosting options today.

Choose VeilForms if: You want PII detection, zero tracking, and flexibility to use any form HTML.

VeilForms vs Tally

Tally offers unlimited forms on their free tier—impressive for casual use. But it's not built for security.

  • Security: Tally has no client-side encryption. Data is transmitted and stored in plaintext on their servers.
  • Compliance: Tally has no HIPAA support and limited data controls.
  • API: Tally's API is limited compared to full REST APIs.
  • Tracking: Tally collects some user analytics.

Choose Tally if: You need unlimited free forms for non-sensitive surveys.

Choose VeilForms if: You collect any sensitive data and care about privacy.
